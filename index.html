Research Projects

Oral Administration of Astragali Radix-derived Extracellular Vesicles Alleviates Ulcerative Colitis

July 2024 – Sep. 2024

This study explored a novel therapeutic strategy for Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD), a condition significantly influenced by oxidative stress. We investigated the potential of extracellular vesicles derived from the Chinese herbal medicine Astragali Radix (AR) to treat ulcerative colitis. AREVs were successfully extracted, purified, and shown to possess excellent antioxidant capacity. In a DSS-induced mouse model, AREVs effectively relieved inflammation and restored intestinal barrier function, providing a new perspective on IBD treatment and a theoretical foundation for using Chinese medicine-derived vesicles.

The Regulation of Tomato Heat Tolerance by the Heat Shock Transcription factor SlHsf14

Oct. 2022 – Oct. 2023

As part of a project addressing the challenge of global warming on crop security, this research aimed to investigate the regulatory mechanism of the heat shock transcription factor SlHsf14 on the thermotolerance of tomatoes. By understanding the function of the SlHsf14 gene, the study seeks to lay the groundwork for cultivating tomatoes with higher heat tolerance, providing a scientific foundation for heat-resistant breeding in other critical crops.

Local Rabies Prevention & Control

June 2023 – June 2024

Despite the availability of vaccines, rabies remains a significant public health threat. This project conducted a retrospective analysis of the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of rabies in the Chongqing region over the past two decades. The research found that while gradual progress has been made in vaccination and treatment protocols, a significant gap remains in public awareness and education. Our findings highlight that enhancing public education is now the most critical focus for future rabies prevention efforts in the region.
